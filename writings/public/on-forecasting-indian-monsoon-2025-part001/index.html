Introduction

In this post, we will analyse India’s historic monsoon rainfall patterns. This analysis is the first step (i.e. 1 / N) to accurately and timely forecast All India Summer Monsoon Rainfall (AISMR) 2025 - and compare it with IMD’s official forecast released in May 2025.

Data Collection

We require India’s historic rainfall data. IMD Pune provides us with daily rainfall data over each \(0.25\degree * 0.25\degree\) grid over India for the time range \(1901 - 2024\).

How big is a \(0.25\degree * 0.25\degree\) grid ? Let’s get a sense by overlaying the map of Delhi and the map of Bangalore on such a grid.

For each year from 1901 to 2024, IMD has provided the daily rainfall data over each Indian grid point as a NetCDF file. On their website we can downloaded the NetCDF file yearwise.

How to download the 124 NetCDF data from IMD Pune’s webpage without manually clicking download button 124 times ? The following python script can be used to automate the download process.

23def download_file(year):
24    data = {
25        'RF25': str(year),
26    }
27
28    response = requests.post('https://www.imdpune.gov.in/cmpg/Griddata/RF25.php', headers=headers, data=data, stream=True)
29    filename = f"data/{str(year)}.nc"
30    print(filename)
31    if response.status_code == 200:
32        with open(filename, "wb") as file:  # Open file in binary write mode
33            for chunk in response.iter_content(chunk_size=8192):  # Read in chunks
34                file.write(chunk)
35        print("File downloaded successfully")
36    else:
37        print(f"Failed to download file: {response.status_code}")
38
39
40def main():
41    years = list(range(1901, 2025))  # List of years
42    
43    # Use a pool of worker processes to download files in parallel
44    num_workers = min(cpu_count(), len(years))  # Use available CPU cores
45    with Pool(processes=num_workers) as pool:
46        pool.map(download_file, years)  # Distribute work across processes
47
48if __name__ == '__main__':
49    main()

How much rainfall did India receive over the course of 2024 ?

The entire landmass of India recived a total of \(6074999.43\ mm\) of rainfall during 2024. On dividing this by \(4964\) - the number of grid points covering the Indian landmass - each grid point received roughly \(1223.81\ mm\) of rainfall.

How much rainfall did India receive over the course of Summer Monsoon 2024 ?

The Summer Monsoon 2024 spans over 4 months:

  • June
  • July
  • August
  • September

Over the course of these 4 months - India received \(4701855.98\ mm\) of rainfall. The area averaged rainfall is \(947.19\ mm\). IMD’s official press release states the area averaged rainfall is \(934.8\ mm\). Thus our calculations are close enough.

The area averaged rainfall received during June, July, August and September months is defined as All India Summer Monsoon Rainfall (AISMR). Thus, the AISMR 2024 is \(947.19\ mm\).

India received 77.4 % of annual rainfall volume during the Summer Monsoon period. This highlights the importance of Indian Monsoon to India’s agricultural, industrial and economic activity.

What is the Long Period Average (LPA) of AISMR ?

IMD Monsoon FAQs defines LPA as the 50 year time-average of AISMR from 1961 to 2010. IMD’s current LPA definition is :

$$ LPA := 880.6\ mm $$

We calculate the LPA as:

$$ LPA = \frac {\sum_{t=1961}^{2010}{AISMR_{t}}} {50} = 850.37\ mm $$

Our calculations are close enough.

Remarks: The LPA definition of IMD is not consistent. The IMD Monsoon FAQs has calculated it as 880 mm while the IMD 2024 monsoon report has calculated it as 868 mm. To stay away from such inconsistencies, we use the Long Term Average (LTA) of AISMR.
